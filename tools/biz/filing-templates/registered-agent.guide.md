# Guide — Registered Agent Designation / Consent

> **General information only — NOT legal or tax advice.** Requirements vary by state. Verify
> with the relevant Secretary of State and consult a licensed professional. You file
> yourself.

## What it is
A **registered agent** is the person or organization a business designates to receive
service of process and official mail. Most states require a registered agent with a
**physical in-state address** who has **consented** to the role.

## The boundary (important)
This tool can **produce the designation/consent document** from your input. It **cannot be**
your registered agent — that requires a live, physical in-state presence and is a separate
service entirely (and is out of scope for this tool, see FF2-4 §4/§7).

## Key inputs (general)
- **State** — rules and forms differ per state (TX-deep first, expandable).
- **Agent physical address** — a real street address in the state (not a PO box).
- **Consent** — the agent must affirmatively consent; many states require a signed consent.

## How/where you file
The registered-agent designation is usually part of, or filed alongside, the formation
document with the relevant Secretary of State. This tool produces the document for **you**
to submit.
