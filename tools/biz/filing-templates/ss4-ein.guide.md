# Guide — SS-4 (Employer Identification Number)

> **General information only — NOT legal or tax advice.** Verify the current form and
> instructions with the IRS before filing. You file this yourself.

## What it is
Form SS-4 is the IRS application for an **Employer Identification Number (EIN)** — the
business's federal tax ID. The EIN is **free** from the IRS.

## When you typically need one
Generally to open a business bank account, hire employees, file business taxes, and to
identify the business to vendors and the business credit bureaus. (In the credit pathway,
this is the `ein` step — gated by having a state-registered entity in good standing.)

## Fields at a glance
- **Legal name** must match your state formation documents exactly.
- **Responsible party** is the individual who controls the entity; their SSN/ITIN/EIN is
  supplied transiently to complete the form and is not retained by this tool.
- **Entity type / reason for applying** — you select from the neutral lists; the tool does
  not choose for you.

## How/where you file
Through the IRS (online application or by the method the current IRS instructions specify).
This tool produces a completed SS-4 for **you** to submit to the IRS.

## Important notes
- The EIN is **free** — be wary of any third party charging to "get" one.
- An EIN identifies a *business*; it is **not** a personal-credit workaround or an SSN
  substitute. (See the credit-pathway PROHIBITED list.)
