# Guide — Texas LLC Certificate of Formation (Form 205)

> **General information only — NOT legal or tax advice.** Forms, fees, and requirements
> change. Verify with the Texas Secretary of State and consult a licensed professional. You
> file with the Texas SOS yourself.

## What it is
The Certificate of Formation (TX SOS **Form 205**) creates a Texas **limited liability
company**. Filing it (and keeping the entity in good standing afterward) is the `entity-good-standing`
gate at the start of the business-credit pathway.

## Key inputs (general)
- **Name** must be distinguishable and include an LLC designator; check availability first.
- **Registered agent** with a **physical Texas address** who has **consented** (see the
  registered-agent template). The tool can produce the designation but **cannot be** your
  agent.
- **Management structure** (member-managed vs manager-managed) — **you** choose; the tool
  presents the options neutrally.

## After formation (general)
Texas entities have ongoing obligations (e.g. the franchise-tax / Public Information Report
cycle) — these feed the compliance calendar (FF2-6). Keeping good standing is what vendors
and bureaus check.

## How/where you file
With the Texas Secretary of State, by the current method/fee they specify. This tool
produces a completed certificate for **you** to submit.
