# Guide — 1023 / 1023-EZ (501(c)(3) Recognition)

> **General information only — NOT legal or tax advice.** Eligibility and substance depend
> on your organization's facts. Verify with the IRS and consult a licensed professional.
> You file with the IRS yourself.

## What it is
Form 1023 (and the streamlined **1023-EZ**) is the IRS application for recognition of
exemption under **section 501(c)(3)**. A nonprofit corporation generally must first be
formed at the state level and have an EIN before applying.

## Which variant
**1023-EZ** is a shorter form available to smaller organizations that meet the IRS
eligibility worksheet criteria; the **full 1023** applies otherwise. The tool collects the
inputs so **you** can apply the IRS eligibility worksheet — it does not decide eligibility
for you.

## Common substance points (general)
- 501(c)(3) **organizing documents** generally must include purpose-limitation and
  **dissolution** clauses.
- The application asks for a narrative of activities and the governing body.

## How/where you file
With the IRS, by the method the current instructions specify (these are typically e-filed
through the federal portal). This tool produces a completed application for **you** to
submit. Recognition also interacts with state-level requirements — verify both.
