# Guide — Texas Nonprofit Corporation Certificate of Formation (Form 202)

> **General information only — NOT legal or tax advice.** Verify with the Texas Secretary of
> State and the IRS and consult a licensed professional. You file with the Texas SOS
> yourself.

## What it is
TX SOS **Form 202** forms a Texas **nonprofit corporation** at the state level. This is a
**separate step** from obtaining federal tax-exempt (e.g. 501(c)(3)) recognition from the
IRS (see the 1023 template).

## Two layers, do not conflate
1. **State formation** (this form) = creates the corporate entity.
2. **Federal exemption** (IRS 1023/1023-EZ) = recognizes tax-exempt status.

If you plan to seek 501(c)(3) status, the IRS generally requires specific
**purpose-limitation and dissolution** clauses in your organizing document. The tool flags
this as a field the **user** decides; verify the federal language against current IRS
guidance before filing.

## Key inputs (general)
- **Registered agent** with a Texas address who consents (see registered-agent template).
- **Directors** — Texas generally requires at least three.
- **Members** — the corporation may or may not have members.

## How/where you file
With the Texas Secretary of State, by the current method/fee they specify. This tool
produces a completed certificate for **you** to submit.
